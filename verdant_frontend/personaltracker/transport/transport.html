How to Lower Your Transportation Carbon Footprint

Transportation accounts for nearly a quarter of global carbon emissions. Cars, trucks, ships, planes, and trains all contribute significantly to climate change. Making smarter transportation choices can greatly reduce your carbon footprint.

Here are four practical ways to cut down your transportation-related carbon emissions.

1. Use public transport or carpool

Public transport like buses, metros, and trains emits far less CO2 per person compared to individual cars. Carpooling with colleagues or friends reduces fuel use and emissions by sharing rides.

2. Walk or cycle for short distances

Nearly half of all car trips are under 5 km. Walking or cycling not only cuts emissions but also improves your health and saves money on fuel.

3. Fly less, choose trains where possible

Air travel is one of the fastest-growing sources of greenhouse gases. A single long-haul flight can emit as much CO2 as some people produce in an entire year. Choosing trains over planes for short and medium distances drastically reduces your footprint.

Example: Delhi to Jaipur

A flight emits around 125 kg CO2 per passenger, while a train journey emits only about 12 kg CO2, reducing emissions by over 90%.

4. Drive efficiently

If you must use a car, maintain it well, avoid over-speeding, and accelerate smoothly to improve fuel efficiency. Keeping tyres inflated and reducing unnecessary weight in the car also helps cut emissions.

Conclusion

Reducing your transportation carbon footprint is achievable through conscious daily choices. Using public transport, walking or cycling, flying less, and driving efficiently are impactful ways to fight climate change.
